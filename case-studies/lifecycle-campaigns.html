Scope

Over two years, I designed every customer-facing email at RVezy — transactional confirmations, marketing campaigns, seasonal promotions, host communications, re-engagement sequences, and referral flows. This wasn't a one-off project; it was an ongoing system that grew with the business.

I partnered with the marketing team to build a modular email template system in Braze. Instead of designing each email from scratch, we created composable blocks — hero modules, listing cards, CTA patterns, testimonial strips — that could be assembled into new campaigns in hours instead of days.

The Lifecycle Map

I mapped every touchpoint in the guest and host journeys where email played a role. The guest lifecycle alone had 23 distinct email touchpoints — from first search to post-trip review request. The host side had another 18.

The goal wasn't to email more — it was to email smarter. We identified the highest-leverage moments: the 48 hours after a search without booking (re-engagement), the day before a trip (anxiety reduction), and the first week after a host's listing goes live (activation coaching).

The Template System

Modular blocks

Every email is built from a library of 12 reusable blocks. Hero images, listing grids, single-CTA sections, testimonials, pricing callouts, and more. Each block is tested across 30+ email clients and has dark mode variants.

Brand consistency at scale

Before the system, each campaign was a one-off design. After, anyone on the marketing team could build a new email by assembling blocks — maintaining visual consistency without design bottlenecks. This freed me up for higher-leverage product work.
